Empowering Women Through Tech: A Tedi Africa Story

Since June 2024, Tedi Africa has been investing in young refugee mothers, working with highly driven teen mothers to motivate and support their lost opportunities in education and business. Our programs are designed to empower women and youth through technology, entrepreneurship, and social innovation.

Refugee Journalism: We leverage the power of refugees' storytelling for social change. Our initiatives have given a voice to many who would otherwise go unheard, creating a ripple effect of empowerment and awareness.

For over a year, we have rolled out a refugee scholars' support initiative in Kakuma, identifying, mentoring, and placing bright refugee scholars at higher education institutions across Africa and the world. In December 2024, we successfully placed one scholar at Harvard University.

Women Economic Empowerment Programs: Since February 2021, we have been involved in women's economic support projects targeting urban refugee women. We identify, train, and place refugee women in entrepreneurial and technological endeavours. We have successfully capacity-built 10 urban refugee women, 3 of whom are now running their own business initiatives.

Research & Innovation: In 2025, we are building a flagship program to help support and ground our work in evidence. The Research & Innovation project aims to support our programs by applying rigorously obtained evidence to support or challenge our current approaches.
