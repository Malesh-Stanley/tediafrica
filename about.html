About Tedi Africa

With a vision to see Africa in which women & youth build just, prosperous, and sustainable communities through social innovation, Tedi Africa is a non-profit, non-governmental, multi-sectoral development social enterprise based and operating in South Sudan and Kenya, intervening in women & youth entrepreneurial space to advance three solutions:

Who We Are

Empowering Women & Youth Through Social Innovation

(i) Provides enterprise support to small, emerging, and scalable entities in the tough business world of South Sudan so that they can stand firm and contribute towards the sustainable development agenda and (ii) Initiate and champion disruptive & innovative solutions to pressing environmental and social challenges that our target group encounters, directing them towards sustainable pathways that are just, socially appealing, and environmentally friendly.
(iii) Ignite, and nurture talent among the youth by availing and creating opportunities for personal growth, and intellectual enrichment necessary for a take-off.

Our Mission

To tackle poverty through social innovation, technology, and entrepreneurship for one million South Sudanese people by 2030.

Our Focus

We focus on refugee women and youth socio-economic empowerment programs: vocational and capacity-building projects in technology, entrepreneurship and educational programs, and youth mentorship programs (both technical and educational support).

Our Programs

Entrepreneurship Development Program: Mentorship programs for refugee youth and women.
Refugee Livelihoods Project: Women's Economic Empowerment programs (capacity-building, and vocational training projects targeting urban and camp-based refugee women).
Refugee Journalism: Leverage the power of storytelling for social change.

Who We Serve

Refugee Youth and Women both in urban and camp settings.

Our Motivation

The core purpose of Tedi is to tackle household poverty and all its ills among South Sudanese women and youth through the use of technology and social innovation techniques. We are inspired by our collective value to co-create just, prosperous and sustainable refugee communities. Our short and medium-term plans pave the way towards that mission. We believe that by leveraging the power of technology, entrepreneurship and education, we can ignite the refugee talent, enabling them to become independent in the long run.

Support & Partnerships

Since 2021, we have entered into several partnerships and received support from organizations such as YSAT Uganda, Girl Power Action Initiative, Global Refugee Youth Network (GRYN), Women's Refugee Commission, One Young World, The New Humanitarian, and American Corner (US Embassy in Kenya). These partnerships have enabled us to launch and scale our Women Empowerment Economic Plan, Young Mothers' Program, and other impactful initiatives.
